1.INTRODUCTION 1.1.   APP VISION This document speciﬁes the software requirements for a Retail Store Management System (RSMS) tailored to a multi-country luxury goods retail chain (e.g., jewelry, watches, leather goods, couture). The RSMS will unify store operations, clienteling, product lifecycle, omnichannel fulﬁlment, and after‑sales services while preserving the brand’s high‑touch experience and compliance with global retail and payments regulations. 1.2.   PURPOSE OF THE APP This project is designed as a conﬁgurable product that can be adapted and customized for various luxury retail formats, store sizes, and regional compliance needs. The primary objectives are to: •Provide a centralized platform for product master data, pricing, inventory, and allocations across boutiques and online. •Enable clienteling (client proﬁles, preferences, purchase history, wishlists) and VIP appointment management to deliver a personalized client experience. •Support retail POS (including split tenders, tax-free, gift receipts, exchanges, returns) with ofﬂine resilience and PCI-DSS compliant payments. •Facilitate omnichannel operations: BOPIS, BORIS, endless aisle, ship‑from‑store, client reservations, and remote selling. •Implement RFID-enabled stock management for real-time visibility, cycle counts, and shrink control. •Orchestrate after-sales services: repairs, services, authentication, warranty, and valuations. •Offer analytics and compliance reporting for sales, inventory, client KPIs, and regulatory needs. •Provide AI‑assisted recommendations (cross‑sell/up‑sell), client segmentation, and demand forecasting. Target audience: Corporate retail teams, boutique managers, sales associates, inventory controllers, merchandisers, after‑sales staff, ﬁnance, and IT operations. 2.SCOPE OF THE PROJECT Deﬁnitions: SKU: Stock Keeping Unit; item-level product identiﬁer. RFID: Radio Frequency Identiﬁcation tag for item-level tracking. Clienteling: Sales approach centered on personal client relationships supported by data. BOPIS/BORIS: Buy Online Pick‑up In Store / Buy Online Return In Store. 
Endless Aisle: Selling inventory not physically available in the store via other locations/DC. AST: After-sales ticket (repairs/services/authentications). POS: Point of Sale system. OMS: Order Management System for omnichannel orchestration. 2.1.   HIGH LEVEL FEATURES ( Add Min 5 features per user type identiﬁed) Sample features shall be as below 2.1.1.Store/Boutique Admin Management: 2.1.1.1.Store Dashboard: Sales targets vs actuals, appointments, staff shifts, stock alerts. 2.1.1.2.Staff & Shift Planning: Sales goals, commissions, performance tracking. 2.1.1.3. Local Assortment & Visual Merchandising: Planograms, lookbooks, compliance checks. 2.1.1.4.Inventory Operations: Receiving, put-away, transfers, cycle counts, discrepancy handling (RFID/mobile). 2.1.1.5.Client Events: Trunk shows, VIP previews, invitation & RSVP management.. 2.1.2.Sales Associate (Client Advisor): 2.1.2.1.Clienteling: Client proﬁles, preferences, sizes, anniversaries, wishlists, privacy consents. 2.1.2.2.Appointment & Remote Selling: Booking, reminders, video consults, curated carts. 2.1.2.3.Assisted Selling: Guided catalogs, look builder, AI recommendations, cross‑sell/upsell. 2.1.2.4.POS & Checkout: Carting, discounts, split tenders, tax‑free, gift receipts, exchanges/returns. 2.1.2.5. Omnichannel: BOPIS pickup, endless‑aisle order placement, ship‑from‑store packing slips. 2.1.3.Inventory Controller 2.1.3.1.RFID Stock Management: Item-level scans, exception reconciliation, shrink analytics. 2.1.3.2.Transfers & Replenishment: Inter-store, DC-to-store, vendor returns; ASN matching. 2.1.3.3.Compliance & Audit: Cycle count schedules, audit trails, variance signoff. 2.1.3.4.Serialization & Certiﬁcates: Serial capture, certiﬁcates of authenticity linkage/printing. 2.1.3.5.Repairs Logistics: Intake/outbound to service centers, loaners, SLA tracking. 2.1.4.After-Sales Service (Repairs & Care) 2.1.4.1.Intake & Diagnostics: AST creation, photos, condition reports, warranty validation. 2.1.4.2.Workﬂow: Estimation, client approval, parts allocation, repair stages, QA, completion. 2.1.4.3.Communication: Client notiﬁcations, approvals, cost estimates, payment collection. 2.1.4.4.Warranty & Authentication: Warranty lookups, authentication requests, valuation letters. 
2.1.4.5.Returns to Client: Pickup scheduling, packaging, documentation. 2.2.   TYPES OF USERS ( Min 3 types of users to be identiﬁed) 1.Corporate Admin / Retail Ops: Manages product, pricing, promotions, compliance; high technical proﬁciency. 2.Boutique Manager: Oversees store KPIs, staff, assortment, events, inventory audits; strong retail ops knowledge. 3.Sales Associate (Client Advisor): Clienteling, selling, POS, omnichannel; strong client service skills. 4.Inventory Controller: Stock accuracy, RFID, transfers, audits; operations focus. 5.After-Sales Specialist: Repairs, warranty, authentication; technical product knowledge. 2.3. Store/Boutique Admin Management: •Display a real-time dashboard tracking sales actuals vs. targets, daily appointments, staff shifts, and stock alerts. •Scheduling shifts, track performance goals, and dynamically calculate associate commissions. •Upload photos to verify localized visual merchandising compliance against corporate planograms and lookbooks. •Mobile/RFID workﬂows for receiving, put-away, transfers, and cycle counts, including discrepancy logging and handling. •Creation of VIP events and trunk shows, managing digital invitations and tracking real-time RSVP statuses. 4.2. Sales Associate (Client Advisor): •Maintain comprehensive client proﬁles capturing sizes, preferences, wishlists, milestone dates, and privacy consents. •Manage bookings, send automated reminders, conduct video consults, and share shoppable curated carts. •Guided digital catalogs and utilize an AI engine to recommend cross-sell and upsell items based on client history. •Transactions processing with support for split tenders, discounts, tax-free documentation, gift receipts, and returns. •Alert associates of BOPIS (Buy Online, Pick Up In-Store) orders and provide workﬂows for endless-aisle ordering and ship-from-store packing. 
4.3. Inventory Controller: •Processing  item-level RFID scans to instantly ﬂag physical vs. systemic inventory exceptions and generate shrink analytics. •Automate Advanced Shipping Notice (ASN) matching for inter-store transfers, DC-to-store replenishment, and vendor returns. •Schedule regular cycle counts and maintain immutable audit trails requiring managerial signoff for variance discrepancies. •Serial number capture for high-value merchandise and automatically link/print localized Certiﬁcates of Authenticity. •Track inbound/outbound shipments to service centers, monitor SLA compliance, and manage loaner item inventory. 4.4. After-Sales Service (Repairs & Care): •Generate After-Sales Tickets (AST) to capture preliminary item condition photos, diagnostic reports, and warranty validation. •Track repair stages (estimation, parts allocation, completion) and mandate a Quality Assurance (QA) checklist prior to ﬁnalization. •Automate client notiﬁcations for status updates and provide a secure digital gateway for cost estimate approvals and payment collection. •Provide lookup tools for active warranties and facilitate ofﬁcial authentication requests or valuation letters from the corporate team. •Schedule client pickups, generate return packaging documentation, and track outbound shipments for completed repairs. 5.SUBMISSIONS 5.1.Code base 5.2.App Video demo 5.3.Memory Proﬁle Screenshot 5.4.Flow diagram
